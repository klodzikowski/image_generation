▸ How does this actually work?
FLUX.1 Schnell is a 12-billion-parameter diffusion model built by Black Forest Labs — the same team that created Stable Diffusion.

The process: When you press Generate, your prompt is sent to HuggingFace's GPU servers. The model starts with pure random noise and uses a Multimodal Diffusion Transformer (MM-DiT) to iteratively remove that noise, guided by your text. Each step makes the image a little cleaner.

Why only 4 steps? FLUX.1 Schnell uses Rectified Flow — a technique where data and noise are connected by straight lines instead of curved paths. This was then distilled so the model can take large steps along those straight paths. Regular diffusion models need 20–50 small steps along curved paths to get the same quality.

The architecture: Unlike earlier models that used a U-Net, FLUX uses a Vision Transformer with special "double-stream" and "single-stream" blocks that jointly process text and image information. The text is encoded by T5-XXL (4.3B params) and CLIP — two separate language models that together give the image generator a rich understanding of your prompt.
